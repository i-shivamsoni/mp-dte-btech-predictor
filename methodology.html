How the prediction works (no machine learning)

This is a deterministic cut-off lookup, the same approach reputable JEE/JoSAA predictors use — not a machine-learning model. Every counselling round publishes a closing rank: the last rank that actually got a seat in each college×branch×category pool. We simply compare your rank to those published closing ranks. If your rank is at or below a seat’s closing rank, that seat was historically reachable.

Each result is labelled by how much cushion you have:

  • Safe your rank is comfortably below the closing rank (≤ 80%).
  • Moderate your rank is near the closing rank.
  • Reach your rank is slightly above (within ~15%).
  • Unreachable well above last year’s closing rank.

For each college×branch we use the most recent year available and the most lenient (last) round in that year as the basis. We never mix the two rank systems below.

The 12th-% predictor first converts your percentage into an estimated qualifying-exam merit rank using that year’s published merit list, then compares it to the Qualifying Exam Based Round closing ranks. The “Second Round” is a JEE round — it is not the percentage round.

Data sources & coverage

  • Cut-offs: 65,600 rows from official MP-DTE opening/closing-rank PDFs, 2017–2025.
  • Seat matrix: the 2026–27 “Tentative List of Institutes and Intake” (674 college×branch rows, 49,391 seats).
  • Qualifying-exam merit lists: 2019, 2022-23, 2024-25 (the %→rank bridge). Other years are being sourced and will be added later.

Category, gender & domicile

Seat pools are written as SOCIAL / SPECIAL / GENDER (e.g. UR/X/OP, OBC/X/F), with a separate home-state field. The predictor uses three inputs:

  • Category (social field: UR, OBC, SC, ST, EWS, FW). If you pick a reserved category you also see general (open-pool) seats, each row labelled with the pool it matched. Reservation appears in the JEE rounds; the Qualifying Exam Based Round is general + EWS + fee-waiver only.
  • Gender — female candidates are eligible for both the open pool and the female-only pool (/F), which usually closes at a more lenient rank; matched female-pool seats are tagged Female.
  • Domicile — this matters a lot. MP home-state seats (tagged MP) close at far higher (easier) ranks than the All-India pool (tagged AI). Non-MP candidates can only take AI / open seats, so selecting “Other state” removes the MP-only seats — including most OBC/SC/ST reservation, which requires MP domicile.

Important limitations

  • Cut-offs drift every year; a prediction is an estimate, not a guarantee.
  • Non-MP-domicile eligibility for these seats is not fully confirmed by this data — selecting “Other state” drops MP home-state-only seats, but always verify with DTE.
  • Fee-waiver (TFW) seats are income-tested; the seat count shows availability, not your eligibility.
  • ~5% of historical cut-off colleges are defunct and have no 2026-27 seat data; they show “n/a” seats.
  • Internal Branch Change data is excluded from prediction (it’s a post-admission swap) and used only for the demand dashboard.
